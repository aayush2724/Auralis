# Pipedrive

## Weaknesses
- Pipeline-centric design: excellent for deal tracking but lacks AI-powered coaching or conversation intelligence.
- No built-in email sequences in lower tiers — requires the LeadBooster add-on (+$32.50/seat/mo).
- Reporting is shallow: advanced analytics and revenue forecasting require the Enterprise plan ($99/seat/mo).
- No sentiment analysis, persona detection, or objection classification — reps are on their own mid-call.
- Limited native integrations compared to HubSpot/Salesforce — heavier reliance on Zapier for automation.
- Customer support quality declines at scale — response times increase significantly above 50 seats.
- Mobile app is functional but not optimised for field sales or quick deal updates on-the-go.

## Our Advantages vs Pipedrive
- **AI layer Pipedrive doesn't have**: Auralis adds real-time objection coaching, sentiment-aware tone guidance, and persona-based pitch angles — none of which Pipedrive offers.
- **Knowledge base RAG**: reps can ask Auralis "What proof points counter a price objection?" and get cited answers in seconds.
- **Single platform**: Auralis replaces Pipedrive + LeadBooster + a coaching tool with one subscription.
- **Conversation memory**: Auralis remembers every objection, tool mentioned, and budget signal across sessions — Pipedrive stores static deal notes.

## Integration Story
- Pipedrive → Auralis: export Contacts, Deals, and Activities via Pipedrive's standard CSV export or REST API.
- Auralis importer maps Pipedrive pipeline stages to Auralis conversation contexts automatically.
- Parallel-run supported: Auralis can post deal summaries back to Pipedrive via webhook during evaluation.
- Migration timeline: typically under 2 hours for up to 50k records.
